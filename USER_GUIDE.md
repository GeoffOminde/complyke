## üìù Creating Employment Contracts

### Why You Need This
- **Legal Requirement**: Every employee must have a written contract
- **Avoid Fines**: No contract = KES 50,000 fine
- **Protect Your Business**: Clear terms prevent disputes

### How to Create a Contract

1. **Click "Contract Generator"** in the menu

2. **Fill in the Form**:
   - **Employee Name**: e.g., "John Kamau"
   - **ID Number**: e.g., "12345678"
   - **Job Title**: e.g., "Shop Assistant"
   - **Gross Salary**: e.g., "25000" (in KES)
   - **Start Date**: Click the calendar and select date

3. **Check for Warnings**:
   - If salary is below KES 15,000, you'll see a yellow warning
   - This is below minimum wage - consider increasing

4. **Click "Generate Contract"**

5. **Review the Contract**:
   - Read through the generated contract
   - Make sure all details are correct
   - Notice the clauses about Housing Levy and SHIF

6. **Download**:
   - Click "Download" button
   - Save the file to your phone/computer
   - Print 2 copies: one for you, one for employee
   - Both parties must sign

### Important Notes
- ‚úÖ The contract includes all required 2025 deductions
- ‚úÖ It's legally compliant with Kenyan law
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Still recommended to have a lawyer review for complex situations

## üí∞ Calculating Employee Salaries

### Why Use the Payroll Calculator
- Know exactly how much to pay your employee
- Know how much to send to KRA, NSSF, SHA
- Avoid mistakes that lead to penalties

### How to Calculate Payroll

1. **Click "Payroll Calc"** in the menu

2. **Enter Gross Salary**:
   - Type the employee's monthly salary before deductions
   - Example: "50000" for KES 50,000

3. **Click "Calculate Payroll"**

4. **Understand the Results**:

   **Deductions Shown**:
   - **Housing Levy**: 1.5% of salary
   - **SHIF**: 2.75% of salary (health insurance)
   - **NSSF**: KES 1,080 (pension)
   - **PAYE**: Income tax (varies by salary)

   **Net Pay**: The amount to give your employee

5. **Follow Payment Instructions**:
   - The calculator shows exactly how much to pay to:
     - KRA (for PAYE tax)
     - SHA (for health insurance)
     - NSSF (for pension)
     - Housing Fund (for housing levy)
     - Your Employee (net pay)

### Example

**If Gross Salary is KES 50,000**:
- Housing Levy: KES 750
- SHIF: KES 1,375
- NSSF: KES 1,080
- PAYE: KES 6,183.35
- **Net Pay: KES 40,611.65** ‚Üê Pay this to employee

### Payment Deadlines
- Pay employee: End of month
- Pay KRA, SHA, Housing: By 9th of next month
- Pay NSSF: By 15th of next month

‚ö†Ô∏è **Late payment = 5% penalty + 1% interest per month!**

## üîí Creating a Privacy Policy

### Why You Need This
- **Legal Requirement**: If you collect customer data, you MUST have a privacy policy
- **ODPC Compliance**: Office of Data Protection Commissioner requires it
- **Avoid Fines**: Up to KES 5,000,000 for violations!

### When You Need a Privacy Policy
- ‚úÖ You collect customer phone numbers
- ‚úÖ You have CCTV cameras
- ‚úÖ You store customer information
- ‚úÖ You have a website or app

### How to Create Your Policy

1. **Click "Privacy Policy"** in the menu

2. **Enter Your Company Name**:
   - Example: "Mama Njeri's Boutique"

3. **Select What Data You Collect**:
   - ‚òëÔ∏è Tick "Phone Numbers" if you collect phone numbers
   - ‚òëÔ∏è Tick "CCTV Surveillance" if you have cameras

4. **Click "Generate Policy"**

5. **Review the Policy**:
   - Read through the generated policy
   - It includes all legal requirements
   - It cites the Data Protection Act 2019

6. **Download and Use**:
   - Click "Download" button
   - Save the file
   - **Post it on your website** (if you have one)
   - **Print and display** in your shop/office
   - **Give copies** to customers who request it

### Important Sections in Your Policy
- Your contact information
- What data you collect
- Why you collect it
- How you protect it
- Customer rights
- How to complain to ODPC

## üéì Understanding Kenyan Compliance

### What You MUST Do as an Employer

1. **Register with KRA**
   - Get a PIN certificate
   - Register for PAYE

2. **Register Employees**
   - NSSF registration
   - SHIF registration
   - Housing Levy registration

3. **Monthly Obligations**
   - Pay employees on time
   - Deduct taxes correctly
   - Remit to KRA by 9th
   - Remit to NSSF by 15th

4. **Keep Records**
   - Employment contracts
   - Payslips
   - Payment receipts
   - Keep for 5+ years

### Penalties to Avoid

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| No employment contract | KES 50,000 fine |
| Late PAYE payment | 5% + 1% monthly interest |
| No privacy policy (if collecting data) | Up to KES 5,000,000 |
| Below minimum wage | KES 200,000 or 1 year jail |
